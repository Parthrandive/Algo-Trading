Executive Summary
This document presents an evolved, integrated trading system architecture designed 
specifically for the Indian financial market. Building upon the foundational framework 
established in previous planning documents, this enhanced version incorporates a 
dedicated Sentiment Analysis Agent in Phase 2, leveraging fine-tuned FinBERT models
 
to capture market sentiment from textual data sources.
The system integrates sentiment analysis with regime detection and threshold logic, 
providing enhanced robustness to volatility patterns while maintaining strict focus on 
Indian market dynamics. This approach is optimized for deployment in early 2026, 
targeting a Sharpe ratio of 1.8-2.5+ in simulations, with an anticipated 10-20% 
improvement in event-driven accuracy through sentiment integration.
1. Core Characteristics
The trading system is designed with exclusive focus on Indian market dynamics and 
infrastructure requirements:
•
Market Focus: Exclusive concentration on Indian market dynamics, removing all 
global market elements
•
Regulatory Framework: Integration of RBI intervention thresholds (inner and 
outer bands) and SEBI compliance requirements
•
Data Ecosystem: Utilization of NSE data infrastructure and Indian 
macroeconomic indicators including FII/DII flows
•
Infrastructure: Mumbai-based deployment for minimal latency with static IP 
whitelisting
•
Volatility Management: Specialized handling of Indian market volatility patterns 
including budget announcements, elections, monsoon impacts, corporate 
earnings seasons, and sudden FII movements
•
Sentiment Integration: Fine-tuned FinBERT model for event-driven sentiment 
analysis from news sources, social media (X posts), RBI reports, and earnings 
transcripts
2. Key Principles
The system architecture is built on several foundational principles that guide its design 
and operation:
•
Threshold-Driven Architecture: Utilization of z-score variables for regime 
switching, now incorporating sentiment quantiles for enhanced signal detection
•
Multi-Agent Hierarchical Structure: Specialized agents organized in a hierarchical
 
framework for optimal decision-making

•
Modern Reinforcement Learning Ensemble: Advanced RL techniques for trading 
execution with continuous learning capabilities
•
Advanced Time-Series & Regime Detection: Sophisticated statistical methods for
 
market state identification and transition modeling
•
Risk-Adjusted Reward Functions: Dynamic weighting mechanisms that adapt to 
market conditions and sentiment signals
•
Nightly Incremental Learning: Sample-efficient foundation models with 
continuous adaptation to recent market patterns
•
Sentiment-Aware Signal Synthesis: Quantification of asymmetric risks from 
textual data, providing enhanced downside protection in high-volatility regimes

4. Phase 1: Specialized Data Orchestration Agents
The first phase establishes the data infrastructure layer, comprising specialized agents 
responsible for data acquisition, preprocessing, and preparation for analytical 
processing.
4.1 NSE Sentinel Agent
Responsible for acquiring real-time and historical market data from the National Stock 
Exchange. Utilizes multiple data sources including nsepython, jugad-data, and official 
NSE APIs to ensure data redundancy and reliability. Captures price data, volume 
information, and order book dynamics for comprehensive market coverage.
4.2 Macro Monitor Agent
Monitors cross-asset correlations and macroeconomic indicators relevant to Indian 
market dynamics. Key data points include:
•
Brent crude oil prices and Dollar Index (DXY)
•
India-US 10-year yield spread
•
Wholesale Price Index (WPI), Consumer Price Index (CPI), and Index of 
Industrial Production (IIP)
•
Foreign Exchange reserves and FII/DII flow data
•
RBI monetary policy bulletins and official communications
4.3 Preprocessing Agent
Performs data normalization, noise filtering, and feature engineering operations. Key 
functions include:
•
Rolling window normalization for adaptive scaling
•
Directional Change (DC) threshold implementation for pattern recognition
•
Corporate action adjustments including stock splits, dividends, and bonus issues
4.4 Textual Data Agent (New)
A newly integrated component that collects and buffers textual data from various Indian 
financial sources. This agent prepares data for sentiment analysis processing and 
handles the following:
•
NSE news feeds and Economic Times articles
•
Social media posts from X (formerly Twitter) via keyword and semantic search 
(e.g., "Nifty earnings OR RBI rate")
•
RBI reports and earnings call transcripts extracted via PDF processing tools
•
Handling of English and Hindi-English code-mixed text where applicable

•
Batch preparation for efficient sentiment processing pipeline

5. Phase 2: The Analyst Board (Hierarchical Modeling)
Phase 2 constitutes the analytical core of the system, where specialized agents process
 
market data through advanced statistical and machine learning techniques to generate 
trading signals and regime classifications.
5.1 Technical Agent
Combines multiple time-series forecasting approaches for robust price prediction:
•
ARIMA-LSTM hybrid model for capturing both linear and non-linear patterns
•
2D Convolutional Neural Network for price and volume pattern recognition
•
GARCH quantiles for adaptive action point determination, establishing entry and 
exit levels based on Value at Risk (VaR) and Expected Shortfall (ES) calculations
5.2 Regime Agent
Implements PEARL (Probabilistic Embeddings for Actor-critic Reinforcement Learning) 
for rapid adaptation to changing market conditions. This meta-learning approach 
enables swift identification of:
•
RBI inner and outer band intervention zones
•
Event-driven regime transitions (budget announcements, elections, earnings 
seasons)
•
Volatility regime shifts and market microstructure changes
5.3 Sentiment Agent (New)
A fine-tuned FinBERT model (based on ProsusAI/finbert) specifically adapted for Indian 
market sentiment analysis. This agent processes textual inputs to generate three-class 
sentiment classifications (positive, negative, neutral).
Training and Fine-Tuning
The model is fine-tuned on Indian-specific datasets including 
harixn/indian_news_sentiment and SEntFiN, incorporating Bayesian priors for stability. 
Daily sentiment scores are aggregated (e.g., mean negative probability) as a new 
threshold variable (z_t), capturing event asymmetry such as FII outflow news.
Continuous Learning
Nightly updates are performed in Phase 5 using recent textual data, ensuring the model 
remains calibrated to evolving market language and sentiment patterns.
5.4 Consensus Agent

Synthesizes signals from all analytical agents using an enhanced LSTAR/STAR 
framework with Bayesian estimation. The agent now incorporates sentiment as a multi-
variable input for improved transition probability estimation.
LSTAR Model Framework
The Logistic Smooth Transition Autoregressive (LSTAR) model is defined as:
y_t = Φ·X_t·(1 - G) + θ·X_t·G + ε_t
where G(s_t; γ, c) = 1 / (1 + exp(-γ(s_t - c))) represents the logistic transition function for
 
asymmetric regime switching.
ESTAR Alternative
For symmetric transitions, the Exponential STAR (ESTAR) variant is employed: G = 1 - 
exp(-γ(s_t - c)²)
Key Enhancements
•
Bayesian estimation of smoothness (γ) and location (c) parameters for improved 
stability
•
Hybrid model selection between LSTAR and ESTAR based on regime 
characteristics
•
Multi-variable transition function incorporating volatility, macro differentials, RBI 
signal strength, and sentiment quantiles
•
Enhanced stability in high-volatility Indian market environments through 
sentiment integration

6. Phase 3: The Strategic Executive (Trader Agent Ensemble)
Phase 3 implements the execution layer through an ensemble of state-of-the-art 
reinforcement learning algorithms, providing robust decision-making across diverse 
market conditions.
6.1 Ensemble Composition
The ensemble combines three complementary RL algorithms:
•
Soft Actor-Critic (SAC): Primary algorithm providing maximum entropy 
exploration for handling regime switches
•
Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO): Ensures training stability and consistent 
policy updates
•
Twin Delayed Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (TD3): Optimizes sample 
efficiency through delayed policy updates
6.2 Decision Mechanism
Decision-making operates through a maximum entropy framework combined with a 
Multi-Threshold Genetic Algorithm for optimal action selection under uncertainty.
6.3 Observation Space
The agent's observation space incorporates:
•
Outputs from all Analyst Board agents (Technical, Regime, Sentiment, 
Consensus)
•
Current regime probabilities and transition likelihoods
•
Volatility thresholds and VaR/ES levels
•
Macro differential indicators
•
Sentiment scores and sentiment-derived threshold variables
6.4 Reward Function Integration
The primary reward function is the RA-DRL composite with dynamic weights, 
supplemented by the ability to hybridize alternative reward functions based on regime 
detection. The system maintains flexibility to adapt reward structures in response to 
changing market conditions.
6.5 Deliberation Process
Before executing any trade, the system performs 3-5 forward policy simulations to 
evaluate potential outcomes under different scenarios, ensuring robust decision-making 
under uncertainty.

7. Phase 4: Independent Risk Overseer
Phase 4 provides comprehensive risk management and regulatory compliance through 
an independent oversight system that operates separately from the trading logic.
7.1 Kill Switch and Circuit Breakers
Implements hard limits on critical risk metrics:
•
Maximum drawdown thresholds
•
Daily loss limits
•
Position size constraints
•
Extreme negative sentiment spike detection (new trigger mechanism)
7.2 Automated Drift Detection (ADDM)
Continuously monitors prediction error metrics. When errors exceed predetermined 
thresholds, the system triggers either an immediate trading halt or forces model 
retraining to address performance degradation.
7.3 Explainable AI (XAI) Logging
Maintains comprehensive audit trails using SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) 
values to identify:
•
Main contributing factors to each trading decision
•
Which agent signals dominated the decision process
•
Which thresholds triggered specific actions
7.4 SEBI Compliance Framework
Ensures adherence to Securities and Exchange Board of India regulations through:
•
Static Mumbai public IP whitelisting for all trading connections
•
Complete order audit trail with timestamps and execution details
•
Position limit verification before order placement
•
Regulatory reporting automation for required disclosures

8. Phase 5: Validation and Evolution Loop
The final phase establishes continuous improvement mechanisms through rigorous 
testing, validation, and adaptive learning protocols.
8.1 Paper Trading Engine
Utilizes vectorbt framework for realistic simulation incorporating:
•
Market impact modeling through realistic slippage functions
•
Transaction cost simulation including brokerage, STT, and exchange fees
•
Order execution timing delays and partial fill scenarios
8.2 Backtest Period and Coverage
Comprehensive backtesting from 2018/2019 to present, ensuring coverage of:
•
COVID-19 market crash and recovery (March 2020)
•
Multiple budget cycles and policy announcements
•
Election periods and political transitions
•
FII flow volatility episodes
•
Recent high-volatility periods (October-November 2022)
8.3 Nightly Incremental Learning
Implements Tiny Time Mixers (TTM) with exogenous variable support and channel 
mixing for efficient adaptation to recent market patterns. This approach enables 
continuous learning while maintaining computational efficiency.
8.4 Meta-Learning Fine-Tuning
PEARL-based meta-learning adapts to the most recent 1-3 month regime patterns, 
enabling rapid adjustment to evolving market conditions without complete model 
retraining.
8.5 Weight and Stability Verification
Automated testing via test_retraining.py script monitors for:
•
Gradient explosion or vanishing issues
•
Weight drift beyond acceptable bounds
•
Training convergence failures
•
Performance degradation indicators

8.6 Sentiment Fine-Tuning Loop (New)
Weekly re-fine-tuning of the FinBERT sentiment model using recent labeled texts 
ensures continued accuracy in sentiment classification. The process includes:
•
Collection of recent labeled data from X posts and news archives
•
Validation against datasets like SEntFiN for quality assurance
•
Performance tracking during high-volatility windows (March-May 2020, October-
November 2022, budget months)
•
Measurement of uplift in consensus signal accuracy attributable to sentiment 
integration

10. Conclusion
This evolved trading system represents a comprehensive, production-ready solution 
specifically engineered for the Indian financial market environment. The integration of 
sentiment analysis through fine-tuned FinBERT models adds a critical dimension to 
traditional quantitative analysis, enabling the system to capture event-driven market 
dynamics that are particularly relevant in the Indian context.
The multi-agent architecture ensures specialization and efficiency at each layer, from 
data acquisition through execution, while the hierarchical design maintains coordination 
and prevents conflicting signals. The emphasis on regime detection and adaptive 
reward weighting enables the system to maintain performance across diverse market 
conditions.
With targeted Sharpe ratios of 1.8-2.5+ and expected improvements of 10-20% in
